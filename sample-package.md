## Narration script

Three simple automations can give a service business its evening back.

First, capture every inquiry in one place and reply instantly with a clear next step.

Second, turn approved jobs into scheduling, reminders, and status updates without duplicate entry.

Third, summarize the day's exceptions into a short review queue so decisions stay human.

The goal isn't to remove people. It's to remove copy-paste work, protect response time, and let the team focus on customers.

Start with one workflow. Measure the hours saved. Keep what earns trust.

## LinkedIn post

The most useful service-business automation is rarely the flashiest.

It usually does three quiet things well:

1. Captures every inquiry in one place and sends a clear first response.
2. Turns an approved job into scheduling, reminders, and status updates without duplicate entry.
3. Gives the owner a short exception queue instead of another full inbox.

The goal is not to remove people from the work. It is to remove copy-paste work from people's evenings.

Start with one workflow. Measure the hours saved and the response time. Keep what earns trust.

## Three X posts
